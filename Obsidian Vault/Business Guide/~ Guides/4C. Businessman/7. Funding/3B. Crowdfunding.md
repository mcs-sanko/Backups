- Type of remuneration
	- Donation based
	- Lending Based
	- Reward based
		- Prepurchase of a product and service. Ship it after.
	- Equity Based
		- Sell stake
		- High risk
		- Basically IIPO
		- Direct share holder (A lot of heads talk to the startup) or nominee shareholder (1 person speaks for investors)
- Money collection scheme
	- All or nothing
		- Only cashed in if a threshold is exceeded
	- Will take your money regardless
- Good for business idea validation, marketing, legitimacy, assessing demand, aggregation of community, getting feedback
- Bad for disclosure of sensitive info, reputational risk if threshold not reached, reduce funding chance in the future

- Equity crowdfunding **Is more suitable for B2C (Business 2 cuonsumer. Direct selling of products) startups that do not need large amount of finance;**

- Checklist
- Before campaigning
	- Pick crowdfunding platform
		- Engagement should be your type
		- Customization, trust etc.
	- Goal & Time Setting
		- Specific and realistic
		- Do not overachieve
		- Up to 30 days is a good time to raise funds. So use your time fully and efficiently.
	- A strong fundraising offer
		- How their funds will be used.
		- Cost segments
		- Convey urgency and time sensitiveness of the funraising
		- Benefits to the donor
		- Move their emotions
	- Introduce yourself
		- What’s your story
		- Your project
		- Timeline, milestones
		- What’s next
		- Who are you, your team
		- Why fundraise?
	- Copy and visual assets
		- Video, images etc. Everything should be concise and everything should give the same message.
		- Immediate, easy, crystal clear
	- Perks strategy
		- Symbolic perks (Recognnition)
		- Material perks (Gadgets etc.)
		- Experiential perks (Events)
		- Perks should represent your idea. Give yourself time and don’t underestimate costs.
