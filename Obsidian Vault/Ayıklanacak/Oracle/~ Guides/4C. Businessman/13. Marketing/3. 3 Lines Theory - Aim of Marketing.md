Perceived Value
Either by branding, creating hype or creating a necessity for your customers

Price
The price you set. You should set it close to the perceived value but cant put it over it. Farther from the perceived value it is a better deal the customer will think they are getting and more customers will buy.

Cost
This is the cost for you to produce that product. Must be below your price otherwise you are in the red.



(Perceived Value - Price) is your Sales Numbers
(Price - Cost) is your margins. Profit per sold.
Sales numbers * Profit per sold = Profit


- Lines can never cross!
- People won’t buy something if it’s perceived value is lower than its price.
- You can’t sell something if it’s cost is more than its price.
- Margin= Price-Cost You want this to be high.
- Keep cost low
- Price high = Perceived Value high
- Market Value=Percieved value-Price
- If something is cheap for its value there will be more people buying it
- Keeping perceived value high
- Exclusivity/Scarcity
- Keeping cost low: Technical
- Making a lot of profit
	- High market share, then you can use economies of scale to lower your cost. (Ford)
	- Low market share, raise prices to meet perceived value. (Ferrari)
	- High market share, high prices (Apple-Philosophical & also sells a lot, **has a following**)
