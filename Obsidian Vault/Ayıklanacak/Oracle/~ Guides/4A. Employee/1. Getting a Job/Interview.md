# Questions You Might Get
- Why this company
	- Do your research find niche yet powerful points that connects you to that company specifically.
- What would you do in the first 100 days of your job?
	- Fact find. Draw a realistic plan.
	- Be humble yet competent.
- What are your strengths?
	- Tailor your response to the role and company.
- What are your weaknesses?
	- Give a real weakness and how you are currently working on addressing it.
- Tell me about a time you failed
	- Tell a real failure
	- How you faced it
	- How you grew through the experience
- Where do you see yourself in 10 years
	- Your ambitions
	- How your growth plan aligns with a role in this company
- How do you handle stress?
	-  Give healthy and sustainable tactics to work through it.
	- Highlight a time when stress led you to rise to the occasion


- Learn everything about the company beforehand.
- Tell me why you are the best candidate:
	- Share EXACTLY what your upsides are.
- Tell them your passions, align them with the company and the position.
- Ask questions
	- What makes your best employee stand out
	- What is the vision of the company
	- Is there anything about me you are unsure about that I can address
- Tell me the story of your life, the decisions you made along the way and why you made them.
- Also some of the most difficult problems you solved and how you solved them. (People who really know their job knows exactly how they did it, they know the little details, half assed people get stuck here)
- Reframe your mindset before intervieweing as: I ve known these people all my life, we are just catching up.

# Tell Me About Yourself
- Tell me about yourself:
	- Dont talk about high school, college or personal details.
	- Talk about yourself in realtion to this job. (How something you do or done in the past has prepared you in parallel to this job)
	- Share your passions, show what drives you.
	- Dont recap your reszume, nobody cares.
	- Highlight couple major accomplishments.
	- Dont talk badly about any past position, manager etc. Avoid negativity.
	- Keep it concise.
- Tell your RELEVANT background in an enjoyable story format that highlights your strengths and uniqueness.
- 1. Start with a unique personal note that gives a look into your personality.
- 2. Talk about your skills connecting to the job description.
- 3. Close it out with what you are looking in this role and marry it back to the job description to show you are a perfect fit.
